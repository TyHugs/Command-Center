Digital Presence Map

A patient trying to find urology care at Michigan Medicine today encounters a fragmented landscape of entry points. Here's what that looks like — and what we've built to solve it.

The Problem
Patients navigate at least five separate digital platforms to find and evaluate urology care at Michigan Medicine — each with different information, different formatting, and no clear pathway connecting them.

Current Entry Points

Each of these platforms serves patients today with varying levels of completeness and connection.

01

UofMHealth.org

Institutional health system site and physician directory. Primary discovery for most patient searches.

Generic templates No dept. identity
02

Medical School Department Site

Academic faculty listings, education programs, and research. Designed for an internal medical school audience.

Academic focus Not patient-facing
03

Specialty Clinical Pages

Condition-specific care guides spread across various Michigan Medicine subdomains.

Siloed by condition No cross-linking
04

Individual Faculty Pages

Profile pages with varying depth and completeness. No consistent format or patient-facing messaging.

Inconsistent quality No governance
05

External Directories & Social

WebMD, Healthgrades, Vitals, Doximity, and social media profiles. Often outdated or duplicated.

No control Duplicate info
+ SECONDARY

Other Pathways

MyChart portal, insurance directories, appointment scheduling systems, and clinical education materials across various domains.

Fragmented

The Solution

A unified digital hub purpose-built for patients, connecting every touchpoint into one authoritative experience.

New Unified Entry Point

Michigan Urology Digital Hub

One destination that replaces fragmentation with clarity — 55 enriched faculty profiles, symptom-based navigation, 7 locations, patient education, and streamlined appointment access.
